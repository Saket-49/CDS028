MYTHS

1. You can get HIV from casual contact, like hugging, kissing, or sharing utensils.

2. Mosquitoes can transmit HIV

3. You can tell if someone has HIV by looking at them.

4. Only certain groups of people get HIV

5. Once you have HIV, you will eventually develop AIDS and die.

6. People with HIV look sick.

7. People with HIV look sick

8. HIV can be spread through saliva

9. People with HIV cannot have children.

10. HIV is only a problem in certain communities.

FACTS

1. HIV is primarily spread through specific bodily fluids, including blood, semen, vaginal fluids, and breast milk. Casual contact does not transmit the virus.

2. Mosquitoes cannot transmit HIV.

3. There are no visible signs or symptoms that can definitively identify someone with HIV.

4. HIV can affect anyone, regardless of sexual orientation, gender, race, or ethnicity.

5. With proper medical care, people living with HIV can live long, healthy lives. Antiretroviral therapy (ART) can significantly reduce the risk of developing AIDS.

6. Many people living with HIV look healthy and have no visible symptoms.

7. HIV is a virus, not a moral judgment.

8. HIV cannot be spread through saliva, tears, or sweat.

9. With proper medical care, people with HIV can have healthy children.

10. HIV can affect anyone, regardless of their background or lifestyle.
